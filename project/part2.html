Lets first explain the reasons of slightly decreased counts. - January 1st: New Years Day - May 28th: Memorial Day - September 3rd: Labor Day These are holidays in America, and this is the reason of the slight drops of counts of flights.

  • The most interesting pattern is that there was a significant drop on number of flights from mid-September.

  • The reason of this would be 9/11 terror attack on the United States, which was on September 11th, 2001. Directly after this terrorist attack, the federal government closed airports, canceling thousands of flights. Even when the airports reopened, airlines experienced at least a 30 percent reduction in demand during the initial shock period. This might caused the airlines to reduce the number of flights.

  • Also, December 24th is Christmas Eve, when people stay home. There is a rapid decrease in number of flights.

  • December 31st, the day before New Years Day, also shows a relatively small number of flights.
